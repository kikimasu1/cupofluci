---
title: Being judgmental can be harmful
description: "don't judge"
date: 2026-07-10T00:00:00.000Z
---

I used to be **very** judgmental when I was younger, before I turned 23 to be exact. At that time, I just feel like people who can't *make it* in life is either weak/lazy or dumb, I don't wanna be like them, that's part of the reason why I worked my ass off during high school. And I took pride in myself for being the mysterious brainiac, with minimium amount of information explosure.

However, as I got admitted to a top grad school, old rules don't apply to me any more. As people **outperformed** me in every possible way, I felt like a *loser* in my own eyes. Which led to sever anxiety and moderate depression, completely took the life out of me. Then I strated to realize how arrogant I was, people are not objects, their value can't be determined by a *small* aspect of their lives (grades, looks, backgrounds etc), and people's value aren't comparable. Also I don't know all of their past lives, who am I to judge? One's judgments only reflect what kind of person they are and their values, can't tell anything about the people being judged by them.

Being non-judgmental of other people frees you from the *fear* of being judged by others, it's a very liberating experience. Give it a try today and you'll feel a big difference in your live.
