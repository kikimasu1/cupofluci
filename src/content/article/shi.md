I was treatly poorly as a kid. Been through 7 years of therapy that kind of poor, having suicidal thoughts as a third grade student that kind of poor, being chased down 5 floors with a belt in my dad's hand that type of poor, being forced to kneel in front of the whole building for several hours cuz I woke up my dad during his afternoon nap that type of poor, being slut shamed for wearing a tilted ponytail in sixth grade by my uncle that type of poor. He said I looked like a hooker, I asked him what does it mean, he answered "ask your dad about this, he knows better".

It wasn't until first year of grad school, where I had a huge mental breakdown, that I realize they were crossing my boundary all the time, with me having learnt helplessness, too afraid to stand up for myself. They all have low self-esteem and are highly insecure, that's why they always want to have power over others.

My parents have a strong perference for son. Me being the middle chid, having an younger brother and an older sister, being the family's escape goat for too long. My sister used to be the one that people can make fun of in the family, now that I was born, she thought she had power over me, can tell me how to live my life, even though I'm a much better person in all aspects. I internalized their criticisms, and feel like shit whenever I'm not the top in any aspect. 

The worst thing about it is I can't find anyone to talk about it. I was afraid of being looked down on by others. I was a timid kid.

The few "friends" that I talked to about this took advantage of me later, assuming that I wouldn't stand up for myself cuz I was used of being taken advantage of. Luckily I cut all of them off. 

I realized that I have power over my life, I can control who I talk to, what things I do everyday, where do I live when I got my first job, that's when I start to slowly detach from them.

I read many books, conversations with god, the power of letting go, the power of now etc, etc. And manga, 能改变我的只有我自己. I realized that only I can help myself, the books, the meds were external.   

As I can interpreate what happened in the past in different ways, I'll always pick the ones that can motivate me do to what I want to do.  Always always look on the bright side. The past is in the past, they can't affect me unless I allow them to. Thinking about future could also be exhausting, I decide to live in the moment. 

What happens right here, right now is for the best. Nothing is a problem unless I think of it as one. 

Let that shit go, grow up. And I'll always give a pat on my head because I deserve one.
