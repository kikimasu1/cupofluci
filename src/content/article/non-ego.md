I used to do things for others. I want to be considered as the most "valuable" person, the prettiest, the smartest, the richest, the most popular one. Even though that's not what I really want, I just don't wanna be looked down on, so I tried my best to show a **glamorized** image, meanwhile minimize social encounters with others as much as possible, in that sense, I'm flawless.

For a period of time it seems to work, as time goes by I start to realize it's not the type of life that I want. I've surpressed my personality for so long just to feed my ego, and this is harmful because I can't focus on what I really want out of life, and the person I truly want to become. 

### So, I took a different approch

When I do things, instead of thinking what others would think of me, I think of me as a part of something big. Like, I'm doing this for the community, for the underrepresented international students, for making this world a better place. 

### Letting go of my ego gave me more freedom than ever

It gave me the courage to follow my heart, since I don't care about other's opinions anymore. Later I realized it can also be preceived as "internal validation". When I approve the things I do, I don't need to walk around *begging* for validation from anyone else. 

My joy/liveliness has motivated many others to stay true to themselves. By being my most authentic self, I'm contributing to the society already. **Simply being has power**, I don't need to do anything to my self-worth. 

I'm very **grateful** for what I have now. And I'm glad I have the courage to follow my heart.  

Live the life you wanna live, for you, and for the greater good.
